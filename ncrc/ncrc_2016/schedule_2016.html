THURSDAY, Jan. 21st

9:00-10:00 AM Registration and Breakfast
A light continental breakfast will be available in the Northwest Atrium.
10:00-11:00 AM Welcome and Keynote from Alexander Kamb
Alexander Kamb is Senior Vice President of Discovery Research at Amgen.
11:00-12:00 PM Workshop/Panel I
Participants attend a workshop or a panel on a specific topic in a small group setting.
12:00-1:00 PM Lunch
Lunch will be provided in Northwest Atrium.
1:00-1:30 PM Plenary Session I
Selected plenary speakers will deliver presentations about their research.
1:30-2:00 PM Plenary Session II
Selected plenary speakers will deliver presentations about their research.
2:00-2:30 PM Plenary Session III
Selected plenary speakers will deliver presentations about their research.
2:30-3:00 PM Plenary Session IV
Selected plenary speakers will deliver presentations about their research.
3:00-3:30 PM Coffee Break
Coffee will be provided in Northwest Atrium.
3:30-4:30 PM Keynote Address: Evelynn Hammonds
Keynote address by Evelynn Hammonds.
4:30-6:00 PM Career Fair
Participants will be able to meet and network with recruiters from a selection of companies, employers and graduate schools.
6:00 PM Conclusion of Activities / Boston Outings
Dinner is not provided by the conference. Conference staff will optionally lead participants on excursions to restaurants in Boston. Get to know Beantown from the local perspective!
 

FRIDAY, Jan. 22nd

9:00-10:00 AM Breakfast
A light continental breakfast will be available in the Northwest Atrium.
10:00-11:00 AM Keynote: Rosina Bierbaum
Rosina Bierbaum is a member of the Presidential Council of Advisors on Science and Technology Policy and has served as former Dean of the School of Natural Resources and Environment at the University of Michigan. She is the former Acting Director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy under President Clinton.
11:00-12:30 PM Research Dialogue Session I
Participants will discuss specific social and ethical issues in research in small working group settings.
12:30-1:30 PM Lunch
An informal lunch will be provided in the Northwest Atrium.
1:30-2:30 PM Research Dialogue Session II
Participants will continue discussing their assigned topics and begin to develop a presentation to share their ideas with other groups.
2:30-3:30 PM Workshop/Panel II
Participants attend a workshop or a panel on a specific topic in a small group setting.
3:30-4:00 PM Coffee Break
Coffee will be provided in Northwest Atrium.
4:00-5:00 PM Keynote Address: George Q. Daley
George Daley is Samuel E. Lux, IV Chair in Hematology/Oncology and Professor of Biological Chemistry and Molecular Pharmacology at Harvard Medical School, as well as Director of the Stem Cell Transplantation Program at Boston Children's Hospital and Dana-Farber Cancer Institute.
5:00-6:00 PM Research Dialogue Session III
Participants will share insights from their Research Dialogue group with groups that focused on different discussion areas.
6:00-8:30 PM Conclusion of Activities/Dinner
Dinner is not provided by the conference. Participants are encouraged to explore the Harvard Square and Cambridge area for dinner options.
8:30-11:00 PM Karaoke Social
Enjoy light refreshments while socializing with other conference participants through a karaoke social.
 

SATURDAY, Jan. 23rd

9:00-10:00 AM Breakfast
A light continental breakfast will be available in the Northwest Atrium.
10:00-11:00 AM Keynote Address: Jeffrey Friedman
Jeffrey Friedman is Marilyn M. Simpson Professor in the Laboratory of Molecular Genetics at the Rockefeller University and discoverer of the hormone leptin.
11:00-12:30 PM Poster Session I
The first round of participants present posters to judges comprising Harvard faculty, post-doctoral fellows, and graduate students.
12:30-1:30 PM Lunch

1:30-3:00 PM Poster Session II
The second round of participants present posters to judges comprising Harvard faculty, post-doctoral fellows, and graduate students.
3:00-3:30 PM Coffee Break
Coffee will be served in Northwest Atrium.
3:30-5:00 PM Poster Session III
The third round of participants present posters to judges comprising Harvard faculty, post-doctoral fellows, and graduate students.
5:00-6:00 PM Poster Breakdown
Participants clear the atrium for Closing Ceremonies.
6:00-7:30 PM Gala Dinner
Participants will enjoy the formal closing gala dinner located in Milstein East (Wasserstein Hall) of the Harvard Law School. Participants will be led to the Law School by NCRC staff members.
7:00-7:30 PM Awards Ceremony
Awards for posters and plenary speakers will be announced following the Gala Dinner.
7:30-8:30 PM Closing Keynote Address: Robert A. Lue y
