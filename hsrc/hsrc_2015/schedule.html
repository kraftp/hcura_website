--- layout: hsrc_post title: HSRC 2015 Schedule --- Subject to change. All HSRC Events take place at the Student Organization Center at Hilles (SOCH) at Harvard University, which is located at 59 Shepherd Street, Cambridge, MA 02138. All participants are responsible for their own transportation to and from the conference.

Saturday, 5 December, 2015

9:00-10:00 AM REGISTRATION
Participants check in at the Student Organization Center at Hilles at Harvard University.
10:00-10:15 AM WELCOME ADDRESS
Executive Officers of Harvard College Undergraduate Research Association
10:15-11:00 AM OPENING FACULTY KEYNOTE PRESENTATION
Faculty members of the Harvard Department of Molecular and Cellular Biology.
11:00-12:00 PM SCIENCE AT HARVARD: PANELS
Participants will attend panels with Harvard students studying various different areas of science. The panels will be conducted in a question and answer format.
12:00-1:00 PM WORKSHOPS
Participants will have the option of choosing to attend one of the following: "Reading Scientific Literature," "Technical and Scientific Writing," "The Art of Crafting Personal Statements and Application Essays," "Public Speaking and Presentation in the Sciences." Workshops will be led by Harvard researchers.
1:00-2:00 PM LUNCH / SCIENCE AND INNOVATION CHALLENGE
A catered lunch will be provided. Participants will also engage collaboratively in a series of scientific challenges that require innovative thinking and problem solving.
2:00-3:00 PM WORKSHOPS
Participants will have the option of choosing to attend one of the following: "Reading Scientific Literature," "Technical and Scientific Writing," "The Art of Crafting Personal Statements and Application Essays," "Public Speaking and Presentation in the Sciences." Workshops will be led by Harvard researchers.
4:00-5:00 PM MENTORING SESSIONS
Participants will have time to work individually with Harvard students to discuss science, research, college, and other topics of interest.
5:00-6:00 PM CLOSING CEREMONY AND FACULTY KEYNOTE PRESENTATION
Faculty Members of the Department of Stem Cell and Regenerative Biology.
